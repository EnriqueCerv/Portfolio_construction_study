Seven portfolio construction methods were compared on a 10-asset universe across for the last 5 years, with transaction costs and turnover penalties. 
We compared HRP, Risk-Parity, Mean-Variance, Min-Variance, Inverse-Variance against the benchmarks of Equal Weight portfolio and full SnP500 (VOO) portfolio.

Across six allocation methods on a 17-asset US equity universe (2021-2026, 10bps transaction costs), risk-parity-style methods (Risk Parity, Inverse-Variance, Min-Variance) achieved the highest Sharpe ratios (1.03-1.12), while mean-variance optimization performed worst (Sharpe 0.64) due to high turnover (10.3x annual) consuming returns. 

Only Risk Parity and Inverse-Variance achieved Sharpe ratios statistically distinguishable from VOO at 95% confidence (block bootstrap, B=10,000). 

However, after multiple-testing correction (Deflated Sharpe Ratio), no method exceeds the conventional 0.95 significance threshold, and bootstrap confidence intervals on individual Sharpe ratios span ~1.7 units — highlighting that 5 years of daily data provides insufficient statistical power to confidently rank these methods. 

HRP did not outperform plain Risk Parity on this small, highly-correlated universe, contrary to López de Prado (2016); we hypothesize that HRP's clustering advantages require larger and more diverse universes.
